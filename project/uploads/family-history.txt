OLUKOTUN  AGURA FAMILY FOUNDATION. 
AIMS AND OBJECTIVES.
PREAMBLE: The  Olukotun Agura Family  Foundation of Okorun quarters Ikare Akoko consists of progenies, both males and females which ancestry can verifiably be traced to Olukotun  Agura of Okorun quarters Ikare Akoko.  He/She is automatic member of the foundation and would be expected to play his/her parts as occasion demands, and shall qualify to enjoy privileges as a member of the gentry.
 AIM
The aim of  Olukotun Agura  Family Foundation is to exist as a viable platform where issues of interest to a member or the family as a whole, are conceptualized, planned and implemented for overall development of the family.
OBJECTIVES.
The Objectives of the family foundation are itemized as follows:
As a rallying platform for all members of Olukotun Agura family, to articulate matters of individual and collective interests.
To foster unity and oneness among members of the family.
To promote familiarity and friendliness among the offsprings of  Olukotun Agura Family, home and in diaspora.
To enhance organized approach to issues of personal or collective interests.
To serve as a body to represent and protect the interest of the members of the family/ family in communal /official affairs.
To continually look for ways and means to chart a developmental agenda for the progress of the family.
  Harmonize historical facts/ family trees, membership database, of the family so that everyone are on same page pertaining historical matters.
Peaceful internal and external conflict resolution among family members or family members with outsiders.
Welfare of members.
Monitoring and evaluation of family members for possible moral or financial interventions.
Creating an organized and acceptable framework for judicious and prudent channeling of funds and resources among competitive interests.
Self-assessment of the progress of the family and members.
And any other objectives that cumulatively promote positive development and growth of the family.
Brief History of the Olukotun (Agura) Family of Okorun, Ikare-Akoko.
The Olukotun lineage is traced to the ancestral Oshodi family of Okorun, Ikare-Akoko. The Oshodi line (Tunolase, Olukotun, Opa, Agbari) descends from Oshodi, one of the high chiefs who followed the Owa-Ale from Enu Owa Quarters in Ile-Ife to establish Ikare land.
Origins and Early Role
During this migration, the Olukotun family served as the Generalisimo—responsible for the security of the people. In that role, Olukotun distinguished himself as a courageous and formidable warlord, always ready to confront invaders and protect Ikare.
His bravery was inherited by his son, whose gallantry earned him the praise-name Amokele (“the one who makes the mountain hard to breach”). After him came Obeeje and Apobiruesin, both of whom preserved the warrior tradition of the family.
> “Obeje, abeno mo Omode o ju” — “the one who draws blood and punishes a child with fire to the face.”
This proverb reflects the feared reputation of Obeeje in his time.
Ikare in the Era of Slave Raids
In those days, the Akoko area was a hotspot for slave raiders supplying the booming slave markets. To protect themselves, the people of Ikare took refuge in the mountains, where cavalry and horsemen found it difficult to penetrate.
Still, raiders sought ways to breach these strongholds. It fell on families like Olukotun’s to organize resistance. Amokele, Olukotun’s son, became the leader charged with defending the mountain settlements. He deployed scouts and guards at strategic points, ensuring that Ikare remained secure throughout the internecine Yoruba wars and the height of the slave trade.
Despite several victories over Nupe (Bida), Ekiti, Oyo, and slave merchants, Amokele also suffered painful losses. Many of his sons—trained as warriors in his household—were killed or captured in battle. The Nupe invasions, in particular, left a heavy toll on the Olukotun warrior clan.
The Obeeje Legacy
As time passed, the family multiplied and spread, but their identity remained rooted in Olukotun. Among Amokele’s sons, Obeeje and Apobiruesin stood out as fearless warriors.
The fame of Obeeje was so great that the family became popularly known as the Obeeje Family.
Obeeje fathered Olorunduyi and Odubado.
Olorunduyi later had Babajide, Olorunfemi, and Olanipekun.
Odubado had Bankole, Asaya, and Adegbola.
From Warriorhood to Christianity
With the decline of wars and the arrival of Christianity in Ikare, the family began to embrace a new identity. An Obeeje descendant, Olorunduyi, accepted the Christian faith. On May 15, 1912, he was baptized Daniel Olorunduyi by Rev. H. F. Gane of the CMS Church, Ikare.
Although a committed Christian, Olorunduyi retained his qualities as a hunter and warrior, and he was highly respected in the community. His acceptance of Christianity marked a turning point, blending the family’s proud warrior heritage with a new spiritual legacy.
Today
From Olukotun the warlord, through Amokele, Obeeje, and their descendants, the family has remained united by courage, resilience, and faith. Today, the Olukotun (Agura) family of Okorun, Ikare-Akoko continues to thrive, bound together in love and guided by the memory of their fearless ancestors.
